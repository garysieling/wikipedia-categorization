Naomi Sykes FSA is a zooarchaeologist and is currently the Lawrence Professor of Archaeology at the University of Exeter.  Sykes researches human-animal relations in the past. Sykes' early work studied the zooarchaeology of the Norman Conquest in Britain.  Her thesis was completed at 2001 at the University of Southampton.  Sykes was previously based at the University of Nottingham, and is currently the Lawrence Professor of Archaeology at the University of Exeter.  In 2014 Sykes published Beastly Questions, which has been described as "a lucid, thought-provoking and challenging review of the state of the discipline."
