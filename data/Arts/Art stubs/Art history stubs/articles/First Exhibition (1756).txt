 London's first ever exhibition of living artists occurred during 1760,[clarification needed] and was organised and hosted by the Royal Society of Arts. There were 130 paintings by 69 painters in the exhibition, including artists such as Reynolds, Wilson, Cosway and Roubiliac.  The exhibition was considered a success.
