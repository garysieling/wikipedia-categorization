The Buyers Market was twice named one of the 50 Fastest Growing Trade Shows in America by Tradeshow Week magazine.  In October 2013, The Rosen Group announced that the Buyers Market of American Craft would be rebranded as the American Made Show.   In 2015 the American Made Show moved from Philadelphia to the Walter E. Washington Convention Center in the nation's capital.  The move to Washington, DC was made not only to heighten the pride of domestically produced goods, but also benefit both exhibitors and buyers. The new centralized location, is more convenient for buyers from the Smithsonian museum stores, national association buyers, art consultants and institutional specifiers looking for domestic and custom art products.
