Jean de Julienne (29 November 1686 - 20 March 1766) was a French businessman, arts patron and art collector, most notable as a friend and protector of Antoine Watteau. He was born and died in Paris. He was the nephew of François Jullienne, who retired from business in 1729 and made Jean manager of the tapestry factory François had set up with his brother in law Jean Glucq near the Manufacture Royale des Gobelins.
