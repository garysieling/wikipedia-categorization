The jurisprudence of concepts was the first sub-school of legal positivism,   according to which, the written law must reflect concepts, when interpreted.  Its main representatives were Ihering, Savigny and Puchta. This school was, thus, the preceding trigger of the idea that law comes from a dogmatic source, imposition from man over man and not a natural consequence of other sciences or of metaphysical faith.
