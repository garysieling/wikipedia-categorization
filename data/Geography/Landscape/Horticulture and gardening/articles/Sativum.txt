Sativa, sativus, and sativum are Latin botanical adjectives meaning cultivated, used to designate certain seed-grown domestic crops.  Sativa (ending in -a) is the feminine form of the adjective, but masculine (-us) and neuter (-um) endings are also used to agree with the gender of the nouns they modify.
