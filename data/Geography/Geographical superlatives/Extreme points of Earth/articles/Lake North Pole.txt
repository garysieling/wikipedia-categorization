Lake North Pole, also known as The North Pool, is a small, shallow lake near the North Pole, and is currently the northernmost lake in the world. It came into existence in 2002, occurring each year, then freezing over in the winter.  The lake, which is approximately one foot deep, is composed almost entirely of fresh water melted from the ice beneath.   A web camera is stationed beside the lake to monitor changes.
