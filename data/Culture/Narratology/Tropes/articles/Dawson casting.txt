Dawson casting is a observed cultural phenomenon and movie trope in film and television where many of the actors appear, and in reality are, much older than the characters they are portraying. The concept is observable in teen dramas such as Glee, and Gossip Girl, and Pretty Little Liars where grown adults are cast to play teenage characters.     The term was originally circulated on the Internet in response to the casting choices in Dawson's Creek.   It has been suggested on several occasions by critics that Dawson casting has several negative implications, specifically for adolescents. These commonly include accusations of unrealistic beauty standards, negative body image, low self-esteem, and general mental health problems, especially in regards to one's self-perception.
