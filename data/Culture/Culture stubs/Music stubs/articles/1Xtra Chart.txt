The 1Xtra Chart was a weekly record chart based on sales of singles in the United Kingdom. It listed the 40 biggest-selling urban music songs released within a three-month time period,  and featured genres such as hip hop, R&B, dancehall and rap.  The chart was compiled by the Official Charts Company (OCC) on behalf of the UK music industry, and each week's new number one was announced on The 1Xtra Chart with Adele Roberts at 1Â p.m. on Saturdays on BBC Radio 1Xtra.  Adele Roberts took over from Sarah-Jane Crawford in spring 2012, and it had been hosted by Ronnie Herel until December 2010.  The chart was also listed on the official websites of both BBC Radio 1 and the OCC. The 1Xtra Chart was launched during October 2007.  Its first number one was "Valerie" by Mark Ronson featuring Amy Winehouse.
