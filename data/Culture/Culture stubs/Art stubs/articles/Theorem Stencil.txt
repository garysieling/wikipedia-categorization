Theorem stencil, sometimes also called theorem painting or velvet painting, is the art of making stencils and using them to make drawings or paintings on fabric or paper.  A vogue for theorem stencil painting began in England at the turn of the 18th century. The art was first taught to women in academies and boarding schools throughout colonial New England. It continued to be taught into the mid-1800s in many other areas.  The designs are traditionally painted on velvet and the work is then framed or matted.
