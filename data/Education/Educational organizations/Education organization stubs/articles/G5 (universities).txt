The G5 is a grouping of five English public research universities that was established in early 2004.   The members are Imperial College London, the London School of Economics, the University of Cambridge, the University of Oxford and University College London.    The members of the G5 initially formed the grouping in order to co-ordinate bidding for an increased share of any extra monies made available in the government's summer spending review.
