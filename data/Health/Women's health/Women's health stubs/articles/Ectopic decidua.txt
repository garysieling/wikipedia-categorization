Decidual cells found outside inner lining of the uterus are called ectopic decidual cells or ectopic decidua. This condition was first described in 1971 by Walker  and the name 'ectopic decidua' was coined by Tausig.  While ectopic decidua is most commonly seen during pregnancy, it rarely occurs in non-pregnant women, accompanied by bleeding and pain. Generally, ectopic decidua has no clinical symptoms, but it sometimes manifests as abdominal pain in pregnancy.  Ectopic decidua most commonly occurs the ovary, cervix and serosal lining of the uterus. It rarely occurs in peritoneum also.
